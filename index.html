Building LEGO Mindstorms Robots

Term 1, Week 2

For one of our IST projects, I helped build LEGO Mindstorms robots for the school Open Day. We set them up to do cool stuff like follow a line, play drums, and row a boat!

Our class put together and programmed different robots. One could follow a line on the floor, another played drums, and we even made a robot that could row a boat. It was awesome to see how coding and robotics can make everyday things come to life in fun ways!

Creating Cel-Based Animation and GIF

Term 1, Week 4

For one of my IST projects, I created a short cel-based animation using Google Slides and made a simple GIF of a bouncing ball. It was a fun introduction to animation!

A cel-based animation is created by drawing individual frames, with each slide in Google Slides acting as a new frame. I used this technique to animate a character or object, making it appear to move. I also made a GIF of a bouncing ball, which helped me understand looping and frame rates in animation. It wasn't perfect, but it was a cool way to explore motion graphics!

Creating a "How-To" video

Social Media Privacy

Term 4, Week 2

We needed to make an infographic aimed at teenagers which highlights what privacy issues they should be aware of, especially surrounding social media companies.

Turns out, social media apps can access a whole lot more information than you could imagine! For this assignment, we needed to actually read the terms and conditions for social media apps and create an inforgraphic based off that.
